Executive coaching is not for all CEOs. And every coach is not the right fit for every client. Here are the criteria that I've found make for the most successful and productive client-coach relationships:

First and foremost, you recognize that you are facing challenges. You feel stuck, like your struggling, spinning your wheels, in the weeds, etc. If that sounds like you, you could probably use a coach.

Most importantly, to benefit from coaching you must have the right mentality, specifically:
  • Passion for Learning (constantly consuming content, reading, watching videos, asking questions of others, etc.)
  • Accountability (willing to put learning into action, to do what it takes to grow and change
  • Believe in investing in your biz and self. (expect ROI, know that growth is not possible without investing in external resources.)
  • Humility of character (realize they don't know it all and can't do it alone. open to receiving help)
I work with business with at least 2 million dollars in revenue, in the area of Palm Beach, Broward or Dade counties in South Florida.
